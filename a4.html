Box design
First, I set a 20mm*20mm square as a basic geometry for the side of the box. Then I took the thickness: 1.33mm of the PCB board into account and subtracted the corresponding depth from the 2D shape of the sides.
The sides are divided into 5 segments for tabs and slots with the width of 4mm for each segment.
Tolerance
I started to test tolerance: 0.1mm, however it is way too loose. I then reduced it to tolerance: 0.05mm, but it’s still not close. Then I tried tolerance: 0.03mm and tolerance: 0mm. While the latter is too tight that requires to be hammered down, the former cannot pass the hanging test. Finally, with tolerance: 0.02mm the box was able to hold itself snuggly and without being hammered.
