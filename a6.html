Lamp and Mold Part 1
Lamp design
The lamp is composed by 4 parts: the electric core, the light bulb holder, the base, and the lampshade. Both lampshade and the base are textured with a weaving pattern. I also designed the holder with a baby blue color to add a little flair.
Behind the scene
One of the biggest challenges I had was to find the right UV counts for the mesh, right printing methods, and right printer settings which contribute to the success and the aesthetics of the printed lamp all together.
After iterating 15+ different mesh patterns with different printer settings, I finally found the perimeters that could produce the desired effects: UV: 200*0.8mm, Layer height: 0.4mm, Print speed: 5mm/s, Hotend temp: 205 and Cooling: 100%. Now each single layer (0.8mm) of the mesh is actually two printed layers (0.4mm) which improves the structural stability and also overall printability.
For the light bulb I initially wanted to use a standard E26 cylinder-shape one, but it was too bright and too long. So I switched to a mini E26 light bulb that would illuminate the entire lampshade in a gentle way.
Mold design
My idea for the mold is to make a Master Sword from the Legend of Zelda. I customized the sword a little bit by remodelling it but it is still too thin which could cause problem when pouring plaster into it. I also noticed another potential issue which is that the “wing” part might need another sprue as it is higher than the main body of the sword.
